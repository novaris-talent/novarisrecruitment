Stage 01 - Sourcing
Finding the Right People
Before you reach out to anyone, make sure you understand exactly who you are looking for. Sourcing without clarity wastes everyone's time.
First step always: Read the Job Description fully before sourcing. Know the must-haves vs. nice-to-haves. If there is no JD, do not start sourcing - ask for one.
Where to Source
  • LinkedIn (primary for professionals)
  • LinkedIn Recruiter / Boolean search
  • Jobberman, Ngcareers (Nigeria-based roles)
  • Indeed and Google Jobs
  • Professional WhatsApp groups
  • Alumni networks and referrals
  • Talent databases from previous searches
  • Twitter/X for niche technical roles
What to Look For in a Profile
  • Role fit - Does their title and experience match what we need? Look at their last 2-3 roles.
  • Industry relevance - Have they worked in the same or adjacent industry?
  • Progression - Are they growing? Promotions, expanding scope, increasing responsibility.
  • Tenure - Check for job-hopping vs. strategic moves. Context matters - ask later.
  • Education and certifications - Only where the JD specifies this as a requirement.
  • Location - Confirm they are in or open to the required location or remote arrangement.
Green Flags
  • Clear, quantified achievements
  • Relevant sector experience
  • Steady growth trajectory
  • Active professional presence
  • Recommendations or endorsements
Red Flags
  • 3+ jobs in under 2 years without context
  • Unexplained gaps of 1+ year
  • Vague roles with no detail
  • Experience that does not match the role level
Sourcing Process
1
Create a Longlist
Add everyone who could potentially fit into the tracker. Do not filter yet.
2
Review Against JD Criteria
Mark each: Strong Fit / Potential Fit / Not a Fit. Add brief notes on why.
3
Build Your Shortlist
Take your top 10-15 candidates forward. Return to the longlist if needed.
4
Share With Supervisor Before Outreach
Do not send any messages yet. Get a quick review first.

Stage 02 - Candidate Conversations
First-Touch and Screening Calls
Your job here is to qualify, build interest, and represent Novaris well. Every conversation reflects on us.
Mindset: You are not just filtering candidates - you are also selling the opportunity. Be warm, clear, and professional - always.
Initial Outreach Message
LinkedIn / WhatsApp / Email Template

Hi [First Name],

I came across your profile and wanted to reach out - we are currently supporting a search for a [Role Title] at a client of ours, and your background in [specific area] stood out to me.

I would love to share more about the role and hear whether it might be of interest. Would you be open to a brief 15-minute call this week?

Screening Call Flow - 15 mins
1
Open (2 mins)
Thank them for their time. Introduce yourself and Novaris. Confirm they have 15 minutes.
2
Describe the Role (3 mins)
What the client does, the role, reporting line, key responsibilities, location, and compensation range if approved to share.
3
Ask Screening Questions (7 mins)
Listen more than you talk. Take notes in real time.
4
Answer Their Questions (2 mins)
If you do not know the answer, say you will confirm and come back - never guess.
5
Close and Set Expectations (1 min)
Tell them what happens next and when to expect to hear from you.
Key Questions to Ask
  • Can you walk me through your current role and what you do day-to-day?
  • What is prompting you to explore new opportunities right now?
  • What is your notice period and how soon can you start?
  • Are you currently interviewing elsewhere?
  • What is your current compensation and what are your expectations? (where approved to ask)
  • What is most important to you in your next role?
Escalate to Supervisor If:
  • Candidate compensation expectations are way above the approved range
  • Candidate asks detailed questions about the client you are not authorised to answer
  • You are unsure whether to proceed or decline a borderline candidate
  • A candidate is difficult or unprofessional on the call

Stage 03 - Scheduling
Setting Up Interviews
Scheduling is about precision and professionalism. Errors here create a bad first impression for both the candidate and the client.
Golden rule: Never confirm an interview time with a candidate until you have confirmed that time with the interviewer first.
Scheduling Process
1
Get Interviewer Availability
Ask for at least 3 available windows (date, time, timezone). Do this before reaching out to the candidate.
2
Offer Options to the Candidate
Present 2-3 options. Do not ask open-endedly "when are you free?" - it creates delays.
3
Confirm the Slot
Once candidate picks a time, confirm immediately in writing to both parties.
4
Send Calendar Invites
Include the meeting link, agenda, and interview format in the invite body.
5
24-Hour Reminder
Send a brief reminder to the candidate the day before.
Message Templates
To Candidate - Offer Options

Hi [Name], great news - we would love to move you forward to an interview with the team.

Here are a few available windows:
- [Date, Time, Timezone]
- [Date, Time, Timezone]
- [Date, Time, Timezone]

Which works best for you? The interview will be [virtual / in-person] and will last approximately [duration].

Confirmation to Candidate

Hi [Name], you are confirmed for [Day, Date at Time - Timezone].

Meeting link: [Link]
Interviewer(s): [Name(s) and Title(s)]
Format: [e.g. competency-based discussion]

Please reach out if anything changes. Looking forward to it!

Stage 04 - Interview Coordination
Managing the Interview Day
Coordination is about making sure nothing falls through the cracks - before, during, and after the interview.
Before the Interview
  • Share the candidate CV and screening notes with interviewers at least 24 hours before
  • Confirm the interviewer is prepared and has reviewed the candidate profile
  • Send the 24-hour reminder to the candidate with link
  • Share the feedback scorecard template with the interviewer
  • Confirm logistics - who is hosting the Zoom room or receiving at reception
On the Day
  • Be available - not in a meeting - in case anything goes wrong
  • Check that the meeting link works 30 minutes before
  • If virtual: join a few minutes early to admit the candidate and handle introductions
  • If anything breaks, communicate immediately with both parties
After the Interview
1
Message the Candidate Same Day
Thank them and confirm when they will hear back. Give a realistic timeline and stick to it.
2
Collect Interviewer Feedback Within 24hrs
Chase the interviewer for their completed scorecard. Use the Feedback Scorecard tab.
3
Update the Tracker
Record interview date, interviewer, outcome, and feedback summary.
4
Confirm Decision With Supervisor
Before communicating any outcome to the candidate - confirm with your supervisor first.

Stage 05 - Making the Offer
Closing the Candidate
An offer is not just a number. It is a conversation. Your job is to land the candidate and ensure a smooth, professional close.
Important: You do not make an offer without explicit approval from your supervisor and the client. Always confirm approved offer details in writing before you speak to the candidate.
Verbal Offer Call Script
Opening

Hi [Name], I have some great news. The team has completed their review and they would love to extend an offer to you for the [Role Title] position. Do you have a few minutes to talk through it?

Share the Offer

The offer includes a salary of [Amount] per annum, a start date of [Date], and [key benefits]. You will receive a formal offer letter within [1-2 business days].

Handle the Response

If they accept: "That is wonderful - I will send the formal letter shortly."

If they ask for time: "Absolutely, take until [date - no more than 5 days] and let me know."

If they push back on salary: "Let me go back to the team and see what is possible. I will come back to you by [specific time]." Then escalate immediately.

Offer Letter Template

Date: DD/MM/YYYY


Dear Candidate Name,


We are pleased to formally extend an offer of employment for the position of Job Title at Company Name.


Start Date: DD/MM/YYYY
Compensation: N Amount per annum
Reporting To: Manager Name
Work Location: Office / Remote / Hybrid


Kindly indicate acceptance by signing and returning this letter by Date.

Warm regards,
Your Name, Novaris Consulting

Always Escalate Before:
  • Sharing any offer details with a candidate
  • Agreeing to a counter-offer or revised salary
  • Extending the decision deadline beyond 5 business days
